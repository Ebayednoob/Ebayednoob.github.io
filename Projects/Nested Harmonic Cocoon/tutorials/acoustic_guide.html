Distributed Mode Loudspeakers & Spatial Phasing

What is a DML?

Standard speakers use a cone moving back and forth to create pistonic waves. A Distributed Mode Loudspeaker (DML) uses a tactile "exciter" (a voice coil without the cone) glued directly to a rigid, lightweight panel (like XPS foam or Balsa wood).

The exciter induces microscopic bending waves across the entire surface of the panel. The panel itself becomes the speaker, generating a massive, diffuse, planar acoustic wave. This creates an immersive wall of sound that doesn't suffer from the directional "beaming" of standard speakers.

The 3/5 & 2/5 Placement Rule

Placing an exciter dead-center on a panel is the worst possible position. A dead-center placement sits perfectly on the vibrational nodes (areas of zero movement) of all even-order harmonics, meaning those frequencies will be silenced.

The Solution: Divide the panel mathematically into fifths.

Place the exciter at:
X = Width × 0.4 (2/5ths)
Y = Height × 0.6 (3/5ths)

This asymmetrical placement misaligns the excitation point from the geometric center, ensuring both odd and even-order harmonics are excited equally.
