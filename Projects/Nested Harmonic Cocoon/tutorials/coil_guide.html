How to Wind Bifilar Coils

Topology, Magnetics, and Construction

What is a Bifilar Coil?

"Bifilar" simply means "two wires." Instead of winding a single strand of copper around a core, you wind two parallel strands simultaneously. The magic happens based on how you connect the ends of those two wires.

By using standard 2-conductor speaker wire (zip-cord), you inherently lay down two perfectly parallel lines of copper.

1. The Non-Inductive Pancake

Wiring: The "U-Turn". Solder the two wires together at the OUTSIDE of the coil. Feed power to the two wires at the INSIDE.

Physics: Current travels outward down Wire A, hits the U-turn, and travels inward down Wire B. Because the current flows in opposite directions in adjacent wires, their magnetic fields perfectly cancel each other out macroscopically. This creates a zero-point scalar vacuum.

2. Tesla's Constructive Stack

Wiring: The "Series Loop". Solder the OUTSIDE of Wire A to the INSIDE of Wire B. Feed power to the INSIDE of Wire A and OUTSIDE of Wire B.

Physics: Current flows in the SAME direction through both wires, but because they are wired in series, the voltage potential between adjacent turns is massive compared to a standard coil. This creates a massive 4:1 multiplied volumetric magnetic field.

Winding Instructions

  1. Prepare the Core: Use a 1-inch to 1.5-inch PVC pipe or acrylic tube as your center core. Cut it to roughly 0.5 inches in height.
  2. Secure the Wire: Strip the ends of your 16 AWG speaker wire. Secure the stripped ends to the inside of the core using hot glue or zip-ties. Leave about 6 inches of slack for your final connections.
  3. Wind Tightly: Begin wrapping the speaker wire around the core. Keep the wire completely flat against the previous turn. Do not let it twist.
  4. Apply Epoxy/Glue: Every 2 or 3 turns, apply a thin layer of cyanoacrylate (superglue) or epoxy to bond the turns together. This prevents "coil whine" (physical vibration of the wires) when pulsed.
  5. Terminate: Once you reach your target Outer Diameter (usually 3.5 inches), cut the wire, leaving 6 inches of slack. Strip and solder the ends according to your chosen topology (Non-Inductive or Constructive).
